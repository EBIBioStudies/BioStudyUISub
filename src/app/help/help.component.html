1. Listing your studies

The first navigation screen shows your submissions in two tabs.

  • Studies: Submissions that have already been added to the BioStudies database.
  • Drafts: New studies which are incomplete or updates of the existing studies that have not been submitted to the BioStudies database yet. Neww studies have a temporary accession starting with TMP_.

Entries in either tabs can be edited or deleted using or under the "Actions" column respectively. Released submissions cannot be deleted. The entire list can be filtered by pressing the icon in the relevant column and providing the text or date to be filtered by.

2. Uploading files

Each user's files are stored in a central repository and made available for all your submissions. To navigate and list all your files, click on in the navigation bar. Click the or button above the list to select additional files to upload to your folder. For large information volumes (i.e., number and/or size of files) FTP/Aspera can be used for uploads. You will need to know your secret directory to be able to upload data into your BioStudies Files area. This can be obtained by clicking on and following the instructions.

3. Starting a new submission

You can click on the New Submissions button to start a new study draft. You will be asked to select the collection that you are submitting to. Please select Default for general submissions to BioStudies. For submissions to BioImage Archive, please read the guide here before proceeding. After clicking you will be presented with a collection specific form for editing your study.

4. Editing a study

In most submission forms, only some of the fields may be required, such as the study's title or its release date. For ease of reference, all required fields are listed under the Validation tab on the left-hand side of the form at all times. Clicking on any entry of the validation list sets the focus on the relevant field, scrolling directly to it if needed.

Some optional groups of fields include mandatory fields, but the entire group can be deleted if not relevant. In the example below, "Annotations" are "Optional" but can be deleted by pressing . Dropdowns inputs in the edit submission form allow to enter new items. This applies to Organism, Organisation and any other dropdown.

  • Look for your item, if it doesn't show up in the list an "Add item" option should come up
  • Click or press enter on the "Add new" item option and the new item should be selected and added to the list

A given study can be edited at any point of its lifecycle. Editing a submitted study will create a new "Draft". Any recent changes in this draft can be cancelled, reverting the form to its previous state before the changes. New fields may be added through the "Add" tab on the left-hand side of the form. It includes the most common field types for a study such as publication tables or annotations lists. In the app, tables and lists are generally referred to as "grids".

If a grid is already present in the form, a new row will be added when clicking on the corresponding entry in the "Add" tab. button inside the corresponding grid has the same effect. Pre-defined or custom table columns may be added too through the button. For more advanced field operations, two options are provided:
  • Edit types: It sets the "Add" tab on edit mode, allowing the change of properties of existing field types. As of the application's current version, only the name can be changed.
  • Add type: It allows the creation of new list, table or section types to choose from when adding fields to the form.

5. Saving and submitting a study

Submission forms are automatically backed up every time a change is made, so you can leave an incomplete form and come back to it later.

Once all the study data has been provided and validated, the form can be submitted by pressing the button, located both in the secondary top bar. If the submission has been successful, you will receive further instructions on the following page, and the accession number of your submission after it has been processed, in an e-mail. .

BioStudies submission tool also allows the automatic extraction of study data from a selected pre-formatted file. This study file has to follow the PageTab format. PageTab files/folders can be submitted by clicking the down-arrow button on the right side of "New Submission" and selecting "Submit PageTab". In case of any issues during the submission process, please contact us.
Please note: The system may take up to 24 hours after submission for any new study to appear in the database. Any study will remain private and accessible only through login until the release date in the Western European Time Zone.
