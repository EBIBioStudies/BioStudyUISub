1.Listing all your studies

The first screen shown is the list of so-called "Submissions". Each of these is, effectively, a form for a current or prospective BioStudies entry. There are two main categories of submissions depending on the stage they are at:

  • Pending: These are new or incomplete submissions that have not been added to the BioStudies database yet.
  • Sent: These are submissions already added to the BioStudies database.

Submissions of either category can be edited or deleted using the buttons with icons   under the "Actions" column.

The entire list can be filtered by any of the data columns. Just press the icon in the relevant column and provide the text or date to be filtered by. Additionally, the sorting order of the displayed rows can be changed by clicking on the icon.

3.Adding a study to the database

Depending on how experimental data is provided, there are two possible ways of adding a given study to the database. Both require clicking one of the two buttons above the list of submissions:

Creates a blank submission form to be manually filled in with study data and added to the database at your own discretion. As part of the process, the project your study belongs to must be selected. This is due to the difference in data requirements that exists from project to project.

Allows the automatic extraction of study data from a selected pre-formatted file. The data can then be incorporated into the database as a new study or used to update an existing one.

4.Editing a study

A given study can be edited at any point of its lifecycle: before being added to the database —as a "pending" submission— or after —as "sent" submission—. Additionally, in the case of a sent submission, any recent changes in the form can be cancelled, reverting the form to its previous state before the changes.

In most submission forms, some of the fields may be required, such as the study's title or its release date. For ease of reference, all required fields are listed under the "Check" tab on the left-hand side of the form at all times. Clicking on any entry of the checklist sets the focus on the relevant field.

New fields may be added through the "Add" tab on the left-hand side of the form. It includes the most common field types for a study: files, links or annotations for example.

The controls allow the addition of sections, lists, tables or rows. The latter can be added using the button inside the corresponding table or list in the form. Pre-defined or custom table columns may be added too through the

For more advanced field operations, two options are provided:

  • Edit types: It sets the "Add" tab on edit mode, allowing the change of properties of existing field types. As of the application's current version, only the name can be changed.
  • New type: It allows the creation of new list, table or section types to choose from when adding fields to the form.
Please note

Sections are not stable in the current version.

5.Saving and submitting a study

Submission forms are automatically backed up every time a change is made, so you can leave an incomplete form and come back to it later.

Once all the study data has been provided, the form can be submitted by pressing the button, located both in the secondary top bar and at the bottom of the form. If the submission has been successful, a summary of all the information pertaining the study is shown.

If the form is submitted with any required fields blank, the application will cancel the submission process and highlight the relevant fields.

Please note

The system may take up to 24 hours after submission to register any new studies in the database.

Please note

Any registered study will remain private and accessible only through login until the release date.
